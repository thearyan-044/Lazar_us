✦ The Project

PROJECT
OVERVIEW

EcoHaven is an award-nominated sustainability and humanitarian design project aimed at addressing the global refugee crisis through architectural innovation. It transforms marine plastic waste into modular, disaster-resistant shelters, engineered to be climate-resilient, transportable, and circular by design. The project is rooted in three core principles: sustainability, dignity, and accessibility.

100% Recycled Materials

Made entirely from ocean plastic waste — giving marine debris a second life as dignified housing.

3-Hour Assembly

Modular design enables rapid deployment — a fully liveable unit assembled in under 3 hours.

UV + Rainproof Surface

Weather-resistant construction for all-season durability in challenging environments.

Climate Resilient

Built for flood, heat, and dust resistance — engineered for the world's harshest conditions.

✦ The Problem

THE
CRISIS

Millions of people around the world are displaced by climate change and conflicts — the numbers are projected to rise to 218 million solely climate-change refugees by 2050.

There are currently no 100% sustainable alternatives to living spaces for refugees. Current accommodations compromise on basic human needs like safety and hygiene.

✦ The Solution

OUR
ANSWER

Our Social Enterprise involves buying plastic from sea/ocean cleaning agencies for dirt cheap initially, and then recycling and processing them through our system of machines to make them into habitable containers.

These can be sold for less than 1% of the price of a 2BHK house and can be inhabited by a family of 8 easily — making dignified housing accessible at scale.

✦ How It's Made

MANUFACTURING
PROCESS

01

Segregation & Shredding

Ocean plastic collected from cleaning agencies is sorted by type and shredded into uniform feedstock for processing.

02

Extrusion

Shredded plastic is melted and extruded through custom dies to form standardised structural profiles and panels.

03

Moulding & Drying

Extruded material is pressed into brick and panel moulds, then air-dried to reach structural strength specifications.

04

Prefabricated Construction

Completed panels and bricks are assembled off-site into prefabricated modules — ready for rapid field deployment.
